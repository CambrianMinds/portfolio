Justin Bogner

Independent researcher. Consciousness, language, and the architectures that connect them.

Nine independent projects published in distinct registers for distinct audiences.

Each piece began as a question I couldn't let go. Some became physics. Some became policy. One became a children's fable. None of them asked for permission to exist.

Projects

Each project stands on its own merits.

Physics · Memoir

The Mathematics of Connection

A memoir of love and loss that reveals a radical physics framework where consciousness is fundamental and connection persists beyond physical separation. It asks: If two minds are truly connected, does spacetime have the power to sever them?

Read

Philosophy · Self-Help

Owner's Manual for the Meat Suit

A philosophical guide to being human in a world that treats consciousness like a problem to solve. Explores embodiment, attention, and what it means to inhabit a biological machine.

Read

Philosophy · Neuroscience

The Serpent's Sentence

A neuroscientific exploration of language origins, consciousness, and the emergence of artificial minds. How do we build minds that understand language the way humans do?

Read

Archaeology · Physics

Resonance Architecture

A family of vibration-mediated physical effects constitutes a coherent technological toolkit. It proposes that ancient megalithic cultures systematically employed controlled vibration to shape massive stone structures with precision.

Read

Investigative · UAP

Notable Disappearances

Truth is not a static state; it is a direction. This project investigates scientifically documented disappearances where conventional explanations fall short, exploring whether consciousness might play a role in these persistent mysteries.

Read

Bioethics · Policy

MAiD Reform

A policy packet arguing that Medical Aid in Dying eligibility should rest on a single principled standard — decisional capacity — instead of suffering-based criteria. Six briefs, one academic paper, and a companion essay.

Read

Constitutional Law · Civic

We Kidnapped the President

A Constitutional Reckoning from Someone Who Was Trying Very Hard Not to Pay Attention. It asks: What happens when executive power becomes detached from accountability, and how does that reshape our understanding of constitutional legitimacy?

Read

Fiction · Children's

Maria

A children's fable about a museum, a mongoose, and the politics of who gets the spotlight. Explores themes of attention, value, and who gets remembered.

About

Justin Bogner is an independent researcher. The work on this site spans consciousness studies, the philosophy of language, the physics of persistent connection, the archaeology of precision stoneworking, the bioethics of end-of-life care, constitutional war powers, and a children's fable. Each project is published in its own register for its own audience.

This site does not represent the projects as a single argument. The pieces stand on their own merits.
