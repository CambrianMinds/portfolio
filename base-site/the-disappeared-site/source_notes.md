## II. THE FOIA SHIELD — LEGAL MECHANISMS

### [FS-1] FOIA Statutory Limitation
- 5 U.S.C. § 552 applies ONLY to federal executive branch agencies, NOT private corporations
- **Key Case**: *U.S. Department of Justice v. Tax Analysts* (1989) — "agency record" requires creation/obtainment by agency AND agency control at time of request
- **Burka v. HHS Four-Factor Test** (87 F.3d 508, 1996): Intent to retain control, agency's ability to use/dispose, whether agency personnel have read/relied on document, degree of integration into agency systems
- **Sources**: justice.gov, bu.edu, rcfp.org, yale.edu

### [FS-2] Food Marketing Institute v. Argus Leader Media (2019) ★ NOVEL CONNECTION
- **Supreme Court 6-3 decision** dramatically expanded Exemption 4 (trade secrets) protection
- **Rejected** 40-year "substantial competitive harm" test from *National Parks v. Morton*
- **New Standard**: Information is "confidential" if owner customarily treats it as private AND provided it under assurance of privacy
- **Implication**: Made it MUCH easier for defense contractors to shield UAP/SAP information shared with government
- **Sources**: Wikipedia, justice.gov, oyez.org

### [FS-3] FOIA Exemptions (Combined Shield)
- **Exemption 1**: National security classification
- **Exemption 3**: Statutory exemptions (AEA, ISA)
- **Exemption 4**: Trade secrets / confidential commercial info (expanded by Argus Leader)
- Combined with contractor-held records problem = nearly impenetrable barrier
- **Sources**: newparadigminstitute.org, aiaa.org, house.gov

### [FS-4] Atomic Energy Act (AEA)
- "Restricted Data" (RD) and "Formerly Restricted Data" (FRD) exempt from standard declassification
- Critics argue used to sequester UAP records by classifying as "foreign nuclear information"
- **Sources**: energy.gov, fas.org, senate.gov, nyujlpp.org

### [FS-5] Invention Secrecy Act (ISA) of 1951 ★ CRITICAL DATA
- Active secrecy orders (trending upward):
  - FY 2021: 5,976

- Prevents publication, prohibits foreign filing, may require classified state

- **Sources**: fas.org, uspto.gov, Wikipedia
